Communicative English (24HS2T01)

📅 Regular Exam Block
▼

Unit 1: Functional Grammar & Building Blocks

  • Subject-Verb Agreement: Ensuring singular/plural subjects match their verbs across complex sentence clauses.
  • Tenses & Voice: Mastering structural shifts between active and passive voice to change the tone of professional writing.
  • Direct & Indirect Speech: Reporting statements accurately by shifting tenses back and modifying pronouns or time markers.

Unit 2: Professional Technical Writing

  • Official Correspondence: Structuring formal business letters, logging system complaints, and managing clear corporate email threads.
  • Resume & Profile Design: Crafting high-impact resumes, cover letters, and professional profiles tailored to tech recruitment.

Differential Equations & Vector Calculus (24BS2T04)

📅 Regular Exam Block
▼

Unit 1: First-Order Ordinary Differential Equations

  • Linear DEs: Identifying and solving equations where the dependent variable and its derivative appear to the first power.
  • Bernoulli Differential Equations: Reducing non-linear equations down to linear versions using custom transformations.
  • Exact Equations: Verifying the partial derivative test ($\frac{\partial M}{\partial y} = \frac{\partial N}{\partial x}$) and applying standard integration rules.
Linear Integrating Factor Template:
I.F. = e^(∫P dx) ==> Solution: y * I.F. = ∫(Q * I.F.)dx + C

Unit 2: Higher-Order Linear Differential Equations

  • Complementary Functions (C.F.): Finding the roots of the characteristic equation to solve the homogeneous side.
  • Particular Integrals (P.I.): Calculating the specific response of the system based on shortcut methods for $e^{ax}$, $\sin(ax)$, or polynomial functions.

Unit 3: Vector Differentiation & Integration Theorems

  • Gradient, Divergence & Curl: Computing spatial rates of change, flux outflux levels, and rotational metrics of vector fields.
  • Integral Integration Theorems: Utilizing Green's, Stokes', and Gauss Divergence theorems to swap between line, surface, and volume math.
Stoke's Line to Surface Conversion:
∫ F · dr = ∫∫ (∇ x F) · n dS

Engineering Chemistry (24BS2T01)

📅 Regular Exam Block
▼

Unit 1: Electrochemistry & Battery Systems

  • Electrode Potentials: Evaluating driving forces using standard reduction potentials and electrode designs.
  • The Nernst Equation: Adjusting cell EMF metrics based on varying operating temperatures and ion solution concentrations.
  • Commercial Battery Packs: Working mechanics of secondary storage systems like Lead-Acid units and Lithium-Ion cells.
Nernst Potential Calculation:
E_Cell = E0_Cell - (0.0591 / n) * log10([Anode Ions] / [Cathode Ions])

Unit 2: Corrosion Engineering & Prevention

  • Corrosion Mechanisms: Comparing direct chemical (dry) attack with electrochemical (wet) degradation cells.
  • Control Measures: Applying protective coatings, choosing robust materials, and deploying sacrificial anode cathodic protection.

Unit 3: Water Technology & Engineering Materials

  • Hardness Treatment: Measuring ppm concentrations and softening water using ion-exchange columns or reverse osmosis.
  • Advanced Polymers & Composites: Synthesis and properties of engineering plastics and conducting polymers for electronics.

Basic Civil & Mechanical Engineering (24ES2T01)

📅 Regular Exam Block
▼

Unit 1: Civil Engineering Foundations & Surveying

  • General Surveying: Understanding measurement frameworks, linear distance tools, and compass tracking configurations.
  • Levelling Operations: Computing elevation metrics, establishing benchmarks, and calculating reduced levels.

Unit 2: Mechanical Power Cycles & Engines

Unit 3: Manufacturing & Machining Basics

  • Machine Tool Foundations: Operational functions of central lathe units, drilling heads, and milling machinery.
  • Joining Methods: Mechanical profiles of arc welding setups, brazing operations, and standard soldering.

Network Analysis (24ES2T02)

📅 CORE ECE TRACK
▼

Unit 1: DC Circuit Analysis & Core Theorems

  • Mesh & Nodal Analysis: Systematically applying KVL and KCL to solve multi-loop matrix circuits.
  • Network Theorems: Finding equivalent circuits using Thevenin's, Norton's, and Superposition principles.
  • Maximum Power Transfer: Deriving load conditions to draw peak power from a source across AC and DC setups.
AC Maximum Power Theorem Requirement:
ZL = Zth* (Load impedance must be the complex conjugate of source impedance)

Unit 2: AC Circuits & Resonance States

  • Sinusoidal Steady-State: Working with phasors, impedance triangles, power factors, and complex active/reactive power.
  • Series & Parallel Resonance: Calculating the exact resonant frequency, bandwidth, and high Quality-Factor (Q) amplification points.

Unit 3: Two-Port Parameter Networks

  • Parameter Conversions: Defining systems through Impedance (Z), Admittance (Y), and Transmission (ABCD) matrices.
  • Interconnections: Analyzing how parameters shift when two-port hardware blocks are stacked in cascade or parallel.
ABCD Transmission Line Equations:
V1 = A*V2 - B*I2
I1 = C*V2 - D*I2

Linear Algebra & Calculus (24BS1T02)

⚠️ CRITICAL SUPPLEMENTARY TRACK
▼

Unit 1: Linear Systems & Matrix Architectures

  • Rank of a Matrix: Reducing systems down to Row Echelon form using basic elementary row operations.
  • System Consistency: Applying the Rouché-Capelli test to evaluate if non-homogeneous systems ($AX = B$) have solutions.
Consistency Rule Check:
Consistent: Rank(A) == Rank([A|B])
Inconsistent (No Solution): Rank(A) != Rank([A|B])

Unit 2: Eigenvalues, Eigenvectors & Diagonalization

  • Characteristic Matrix Equations: Solving $|A - \lambda I| = 0$ to calculate eigenvalues and finding corresponding spatial eigenvectors.
  • Cayley-Hamilton Theorem: Verifying that a matrix satisfies its own characteristic loop to quickly calculate powers or matrix inverses ($A^{-1}$).

Unit 3: Single Variable Calculus & Mean Value Theorems

  • Mean Value Theorems: Mathematical proofs and application of Rolle's, Lagrange's, and Cauchy's Mean Value theorems.
  • Taylor & Maclaurin Series: Expanding continuous functions into polynomial power series centered around zero or custom coordinates.
