Communicative English (24HS2T01)

📅 Exam: Mon, 16-06-2025 | Time: 10:00 AM - 01:00 PM
▼

Unit 1: Lesson Reflection & Basic Structural Grammar

  • Textual Analysis: Critical insights from designated essays/lessons ("Human Values" & "The Cyber Age").
  • Subject-Verb Agreement: Rules of concord, collective nouns, compound subjects, and structural anomalies.
  • Noun-Pronoun Agreement: Maintaining case, gender, and numeric consistency across relative and absolute clauses.
  • Articles & Prepositions: Definite/indefinite structural deployment and complex spatial/temporal prepositions.

Unit 2: Word Mechanics & Sentence Dynamics

  • Active vs. Passive Voice: Structural transformation rules, impersonal passive styles for technical reporting.
  • Direct & Indirect Speech: Tense shifting, conversational reporting layouts, and imperative transformation.
  • Vocabulary Expansion: Etymology bases, roots, common prefixes/suffixes, and contextual expression.
  • Synonyms, Antonyms, Idioms: Academic phrasing arrays and expressions standard in workplace communication.

Unit 3: Textual Cohesion & Reading Mastery

  • Reading Comprehension: Scanning, skimming, structural inferencing, and vocabulary deduction from unknown text blocks.
  • Cohesive Elements: Proper integration of linkers, transitional phrases, and paragraph anchors.
  • Sentence Correction Mechanics: Identifying redundant phrases, misplaced modulators, and syntax violations.

Unit 4: Corporate Correspondence & Technical Drafts

  • Formal Letters: Structuring formal business complaints, inquiries, quotation requests, and official notices.
  • Email Etiquette: Header construction, signature blocks, professional brevity, and file enclosure practices.
  • Technical Summary Writing: Paraphrasing large technical papers while retaining critical structural data.

Unit 5: Advanced Career Composition

  • Resume & CV Architecture: Structuring functional, chronological, and hybrid tech industry resumes.
  • Cover Letter Design: Writing application narratives that match technical skills to job descriptions.
  • Professional Profile Writing: Constructing clean summaries for job portals and professional networking platforms.

Differential Equations & Vector Calculus (24BS2T04)

📅 Exam: Wed, 18-06-2025 | Time: 10:00 AM - 01:00 PM
▼

Unit 1: First-Order Ordinary Differential Equations

  • Exact Equations: Verification via $\frac{\partial M}{\partial y} = \frac{\partial N}{\partial x}$ and applying integration rules.
  • Integrating Factors: Finding I.F. via inspection or formulas for non-exact equations.
  • Linear & Bernoulli DEs: Converting non-linear forms using $v = y^{1-n}$ and solving via integrating factors.
  • Engineering Applications: Modelling Newton’s law of cooling, orthogonal trajectories, and simple exponential growth/decay paths.
Integrating Factor (Linear): I.F. = e^(∫P dx) ==> Solution: y * I.F. = ∫(Q * I.F.)dx + C

Unit 2: Higher-Order Linear Differential Equations (Homogeneous & Non-Homogeneous)

  • Complementary Functions: Real/distinct, complex, or repeated root analysis of the auxiliary equation.
  • Particular Integrals (Shortcut Methods): Solving for non-homogeneous terms matching $e^{ax}$, $\sin(ax)$, $\cos(ax)$, $x^m$, or $e^{ax}V(x)$.
  • Method of Variation of Parameters: Finding general solutions for non-homogeneous equations using Wronskian determinants.
  • LCR Circuit Applications: Setting up and solving second-order equations for voltage and current transient states.
Wronskian Determinant: W = |y1 y2; y1' y2'| ==> P.I. = -y1∫(y2*R)/W dx + y2∫(y1*R)/W dx

Unit 3: Partial Differential Equations & Multivariable Optimization

  • Formation of PDEs: Eliminating arbitrary constants and arbitrary functions from physical surfaces.
  • First-Order Linear PDEs: Setting up and solving Lagrange's linear equations ($Pp + Qq = R$).
  • Multivariable Extremas: Applying partial derivatives to determine maxima, minima, and saddle points via the discriminant check ($rt - s^2$).

Unit 4: Vector Differentiation & Kinematics

  • Vector Scalar Fields: Tracking position vectors, velocities, and acceleration components along space curves.
  • Gradient & Directional Derivatives: Calculating maximum rates of change and standard normals to level surfaces ($\nabla \phi$).
  • Divergence & Curl operators: Physical interpretations of fluid flux ($\nabla \cdot \mathbf{F}$) and rotational vorticity ($\nabla \times \mathbf{F}$).
  • Solenoidal & Irrotational Fields: Solenoidal proof ($\nabla \cdot \mathbf{F} = 0$) and conservative scalar potential tracking ($\nabla \times \mathbf{F} = \mathbf{0}$).

Unit 5: Vector Integration Theorems

  • Line, Surface, & Volume Integrals: Work done calculations, mass densities, and fluid outflux computations over bounding limits.
  • Green's Theorem in a Plane: Converting multi-variable closed line paths to standard double integrals over bounded 2D surfaces.
  • Stokes' Theorem: Swapping line integrals of vector boundaries with open surface flux integrals of their curl.
  • Gauss Divergence Theorem: Evaluating net outward flux through a closed spatial surface via direct 3D volume integration.
Gauss Divergence Theorem: ∯ F · n dS = ∭ (∇ · F) dV

Engineering Chemistry (24BS2T01)

📅 Exam: Fri, 20-06-2025 | Time: 10:00 AM - 01:00 PM
▼

Unit 1: Electrochemistry & Battery Technologies

  • Electrochemical Cells: Galvanic cell setup, liquid junction potential anomalies, and reference electrode designs (Calomel, Ag/AgCl).
  • Nernst Equation: Cell potential modifications based on concentration and temperature shifts.
  • Primary & Secondary Batteries: Architectures of Zinc-Air, Lead-Acid, Nickel-Cadmium, and Lithium-Ion battery configurations.
  • Fuel Cells: Clean energy operations using Hydrogen-Oxygen and Methanol-Oxygen fuel cell interfaces.
Nernst Cell Calculation: E = E0 - (0.0591 / n) * log10([Anode / Cathode])

Unit 2: Corrosion Dynamics & Materials Protection

  • Corrosion Mechanisms: Dry chemical corrosion vs. wet electrochemical corrosion (Galvanic, Concentration cells).
  • Corrosion Modifiers: Influence of pH, temperature, dissolved oxygen levels, and relative metal surface area ratios.
  • Cathodic Protection Systems: Sacrificial anode mechanisms and continuous Impressed Current Cathodic Protection (ICCP).
  • Surface Finish Coatings: Electroplating (Nickel/Copper), electroless deposition, and hot-dipping procedures (Galvanizing/Tinning).

Unit 3: Modern Engineering Materials & Polymers

  • Polymer Foundations: Thermoplastics vs. Thermosetting plastics; free radical addition mechanisms.
  • Industrial Elastomers: Preparation, properties, and applications of Buna-S, Buna-N, and Thiokol rubbers.
  • Conducting Polymers: Doping mechanisms and charge transport pathways in Polyacetylene and Polyaniline for electronic applications.
  • Nano-Structured Materials: Carbon Nanotubes (CNTs) and Graphene synthesis via Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD).

Unit 4: Spectroscopic Analytical Techniques

  • Electromagnetic Radiation: Regions of the spectrum, energy quantization rules, and molecular absorption dynamics.
  • UV-Visible Spectroscopy: Electronic transitions, Beer-Lambert absorption law rules, instrumentation layouts, and applications.
  • FTIR Spectroscopy: Molecular vibration frequencies, functional group mapping, and structural configuration tracking.
  • Chromatography Basics: Separation mechanics in Gas Chromatography (GC) and High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC).

Unit 5: Water Technology & Industrial Management

  • Water Impurities & Hardness: Temporary vs. permanent hardness, ppm/EDTA titration tracking calculations.
  • Boiler Complications: Scales, sludges, caustic embrittlement, and boiler corrosion failures.
  • External Conditioning: Demineralization (Ion-Exchange resin systems) and Zeolite softening setups.
  • Desalination Methods: Reverse Osmosis (RO) thin-film membranes and electrodialysis filtration loops.

📅 Exam: Mon, 23-06-2025 | Time: 10:00 AM - 01:00 PM

Unit 1: Civil Engineering Scope & Geodetic Surveying

  • Civil Disciplines: Structural, geotechnical, water resources, environmental, and transportation domains.
  • Surveying Principles: Linear measurements, chain configurations, compass traversing, and error minimization.
  • Levelling Systems: Benchmarks, Height of Instrument methods, Rise and Fall calculations, and field adjustments.
Height of Instrument (HI) = Elevation + Backsight (BS)
True Elevation = HI - Foresight (FS)

Unit 2: Infrastructure Materials & Components

  • Building Materials: Selection parameters for bricks, cement classification types, aggregates, and smart concrete mixes.
  • Structural Elements: Shallow/deep foundations, load-bearing masonry walls, columns, beams, and roof slabs.
  • Urban Infrastructure: Smart city traffic structures, clean water storage distribution, and wastewater treatment systems.

Unit 3: Mechanical Engineering Foundations & Thermal Systems

  • Mechanical Overview: Manufacturing systems, robotics, machine tools, and power generation loops.
  • Thermodynamics Foundations: Laws of thermodynamics, properties of pure states, and standard working gases.
  • Steam Generation & Power: Layouts of thermal power facilities, boilers, water turbines, and power outputs.

Unit 4: Power Cycles & Energy Conversion

  • Internal Combustion Engines: Comparative mechanics of 2-Stroke vs. 4-Stroke and Spark Ignition (SI) vs. Compression Ignition (CI).
  • Air-Standard Cycles: Thermal efficiency calculations for the Otto, Diesel, and dual combustion cycles.
  • Refrigeration & Heat Pumps: Vapor Compression Refrigeration System (VCRS) layouts and eco-friendly refrigerant selections.

Unit 5: Manufacturing Technologies & Machine Tools

  • Machining Foundations: Operational kinematics of the lathe, drilling heads, milling fixtures, and grinding systems.
  • Metal Joining Methods: Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW), gas welding configurations, brazing, and electronic soldering.
  • Advanced Manufacturing: Overview of CNC setups, automated assembly lines, and 3D printing/additive production systems.

Network Analysis (24ES2T02)

📅 Exam: Wed, 25-06-2025 | Time: 10:00 AM - 01:00 PM
▼

Unit 1: Fundamental DC Circuit Analysis

  • Network Elements: Active/passive, linear/non-linear, unilateral/bilateral, and lumped/distributed parameter spaces.
  • Source Transformations: Shifting independent/dependent voltage and current source networks.
  • Mesh & Nodal Formulations: Systematically applying KVL and KCL to complex multi-loop circuits with supermesh/supernode configurations.
  • Star-Delta Conversions: Network reductions using standard balance formulas.

Unit 2: Core Network Theorems (DC & AC Contexts)

  • Superposition & Thevenin's Theorems: Linear extraction of multi-source topologies and open-circuit voltage calculations.
  • Norton's & Millman's Theorems: Determining short-circuit current matrices and equivalent terminal configurations.
  • Maximum Power Transfer Theorem: Deriving load conditions to draw peak power across AC (complex conjugate) and DC setups.
  • Reciprocity & Substitution: Boundary condition mapping and invariance proofs for bilateral passive networks.
AC Max Power Condition: Z_Load = Z_Thevenin* (Complex Conjugate Match)

Unit 3: Single-Phase AC Steady-State Analysis

  • Sinusoidal Waveforms: Calculating RMS values, average values, form factor, and peak factor profiles.
  • Phasor Representation: Imaginary notation ($j$), phase angle tracking, complex impedance triangles, and admittance spaces.
  • Power Configurations: Distinguishing active power ($P$), reactive power ($Q$), apparent power ($S$), and power factor correction metrics.
  • Steady State Analysis: Current tracking through series and parallel RLC networks.

Unit 4: Resonance States & Magnetically Coupled Networks

  • Series & Parallel Resonance: Resonant frequency formulas, half-power points, bandwidth calculations, and Quality-Factor (Q) selectivity metrics.
  • Magnetic Coupling: Self-inductance ($L$), mutual inductance ($M$), coupling coefficient calculation ($k$), and dot convention polarity tracking.
  • Tuned Circuit Topologies: Single-tuned transformer loops and primary/secondary circuit responses.
Resonant Frequency: f_r = 1 / (2 * π * √(L * C))
Quality Factor: Q = ω_r * L / R = 1 / (ω_r * C * R)

Unit 5: Two-Port Parameter Networks & Transients

  • Parameter Configurations: Extracting Open Circuit Impedance ($Z$), Short Circuit Admittance ($Y$), Hybrid ($h$), and Transmission ($ABCD$) parameters.
  • Interconnections & Conditions: Inter-parameter conversion matrices, symmetry checks, and reciprocity proofs.
  • Transient Foundations: Initial and final boundary condition states for R-L and R-C networks under DC switching actions.

Linear Algebra & Calculus (24BS1T02)

⚠️ Supplementary Track (JNTUK Supplementary Calendar)
▼

Unit 1: Linear Systems & Matrix Operations

  • Rank of a Matrix: Computing row/column spaces using elementary row operations and Row Echelon reductions.
  • Systems of Equations: Solving non-homogeneous systems ($AX = B$) via the Rouché-Capelli consistency test.
  • Homogeneous Systems: Finding trivial and non-trivial solution vectors for the system $AX = \mathbf{0}$.
  • Numerical Solvers: Finding solutions via Gauss Elimination and Gauss-Jordan matrix reduction processes.
Rouché-Capelli Rule:
Consistent: Rank(A) == Rank([A|B])
Inconsistent (No Solution): Rank(A) != Rank([A|B])

Unit 2: Linear Transformations & Matrix Diagonalization

  • Eigenvalues & Eigenvectors: Roots of the characteristic matrix equation ($|A - \lambda I| = 0$) and spatial orientation tracking.
  • Cayley-Hamilton Theorem: Verification proofs and applications for calculating matrix powers ($A^n$) or inverse models ($A^{-1}$).
  • Diagonalization: Constructing modal matrices ($P$) and processing similarity transformations ($P^{-1}AP = D$).
  • Quadratic Forms: Converting quadratic expressions to canonical forms via orthogonal transformations and verifying definite signatures.

Unit 3: Single Variable Calculus & Mean Value Calculus

  • Mean Value Theorems: Proofs and applications of Rolle's, Lagrange's, and Cauchy's Mean Value theorems.
  • Taylor & Maclaurin Series: Building polynomial approximations for transcendental functions around localized convergence limits.
  • Indeterminate Forms: Evaluating limits via L’Hôpital’s rule across fractions and exponential powers.

Unit 4: Multivariable Differential Calculus

  • Partial Derivatives: First-order, higher-order partial parameters, and chain rule structures for composite functions.
  • Total Derivatives: Evaluating complete differentials and directional error tracking metrics.
  • Euler's Theorem: Homogeneous function checks and mathematical proofs using degrees of homogeneity.
  • Jacobian Transformations: Determinant coordinate transformations for Cartesian, polar, and spherical frameworks.

Unit 5: Multivariable Integral Calculus

  • Double Integrals: Evaluation over rectangular boundaries and non-uniform regions.
  • Change of Order of Integration: Modifying integration sequences to evaluate complex bounded 2D surfaces.
  • Triple Integrals: Volume computations within bounding planes in 3D space.
  • Coordinate Transformations: Swapping Cartesian triple systems over to easier cylindrical and spherical coordinate integrations.
